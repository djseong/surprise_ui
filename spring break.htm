3/13/2018

Snowstorm today so spent the entire day in the house. I woke up at 10 and spent an hour reading the Bible, doing a devotional, and thinking about Michel. I read Romans 1-5 because I remember I really felt convicted from reading Romans in middle school. Reading it again though Romans is pretty complex so I don’t think I really understood most of it in middle school. I really liked chapter 4 that talks about how Abraham was a model of someone who was deemed righteous through his faith and not by his works. Verse 4 says how someone who works will get wages as an obligation, but someone who has faith in God will get righteousness as a gift. I think it was a good reminder to not try to earn righteousness through the law but seek the righteousness that comes through faith and accept it as a gift from God.

The devotional I read was about how to develop a healthy Christian relationship and it was pretty unhelpful. I think the main point the writer was trying to make was to not have sex. He also said though that honesty was a key characteristic of a healthy relationship. I’ll keep that in mind.

I spent the rest of the time thinking about Michel and I think I feel better about our relationship. To be honest I didn’t expect that I would have this amount of fear going into the relationship so processing that has thrown me off a bit. Forcing myself to think has really helped though and I’m glad I had the time to do it this break. I feel like I’m getting to a place where I can focus my attention on loving Michel the way God loves her in the moment and not be tied up by thoughts of if I’m doing the right thing. It’s a fine balance between the two because I don’t want to disregard thoughts of doing the right thing but at the same time I don’t want those thoughts to make me anxious. I’m glad Michel told me to just force myself to think and process it. I’m also really glad our relationship has its foundation on God. Even though it can get kind of messy I can also start my thoughts on that foundation and have faith that those thoughts are going in the right direction.
